Student-Led Teaching Awards

Has a staff member you know gone above and beyond for you or your coursemates? Then nominate them for a Student-led Teaching Award.

Our student-led teaching awards celebrate the work of exceptional staff across NTU who work tirelessly to improve the student experience, provide excellent teaching, and develop students to their full potential.

You can nominate staff members in the following categories:

Has a staff member ever gone above and beyond to support your personal wellbeing?   Perhaps you have a tutor who consistently recognises student welfare as a key priority? This award will consider, but is not limited to, the following criteria:

  • The nominee aids the transition process into university life.

  • The nominee demonstrates an awareness of the link between student welfare and academic performance, and as such, seeks to support student welfare so to foster academic achievement.

  • The nominee is aware of the appropriate referral processes and uses them accordingly.

  • The nominee understands the importance of, and looks to develop, a strong sense of community amongst NTU students.

 

Is there a staff member who has contributed significantly to your student experience and positively impacted your time at NTU? This award seeks to celebrate those staff members and their continued efforts by highlighting the contributions they make to creating an excellent student experience. This could be anyone from the administrators in your department to the technicians in your lab. 

Please note: This is not an award for academic teaching staff. Lecturers/seminar leaders will be automatically removed from this category. 
This award will consider, but is not limited to, the following criteria:

  • The nominee may have assisted in the development of student study skills.

  • The nominee develops good relationships with students, recognising their individual needs.

  • The nominee has positively impacted students' time at NTU, contributing to their overall experience.

  • The nominee supports opportunities for students and enhances their potential outside of the classroom.

 

 

Do you know a staff member who proactively works to remove barriers for students, and encourages a sense of belonging within their teaching? Perhaps they lead on an initiative to increase equity in their discipline or have been pivotal in initiating conversations on diversity and inclusion on campus and improving education around these topics

For this award, we will consider, but not be limited to, the following criteria:  

  • The nominee engages all students, reaching traditionally underrepresented demographics/groups

  • The nominee will recognise the importance of increasing equity and accessibility whilst making, or lobbying for, adjustments to teaching and learning, such that students are provided with the best opportunities possible for success.

  • The nominee will ensure the curriculum, related resources and any guest speakers reflect the diverse history of, and contributions to, the discipline.

  • The nominee will consider diversity and inclusion holistically to create a sense of belonging.

  • The nominee will be an approachable individual, engaging in productive or educational discussion frequently on campus in addition to their curriculum teaching.

  • The nominee will themselves, or encourage others to, engage in potentially uncomfortable conversations for them to reflect and learn.

  • The nominee will be perceptive of social justice movements such as Black Lives Matter and #MeToo and encourage education in a context beyond the immediate NTU community.

 

Are you taught by an academic who demonstrates a commitment to sustainability? Sustainability refers to ‘fulfilling the needs of current generations without compromising the needs of future generations, while ensuring a balance between economic growth, environmental care and social well-being’.  Commitment to sustainability must extend beyond a simple consideration for environmental impacts, incorporating societal and economic factors as well. This award seeks to commend those academic staff who embody this commitment to sustainability.

This award will consider, but is not limited to, the following criteria:

  • The nominee encourages discussion over issues of sustainability. These discussions may draw on conceptual frameworks such as the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals to achieve this.

  • The nominee encourages students to consider their field of study in relation to wider sustainability concerns.

  • The nominee continually updates their course content in line with emerging theories and literature, so to prioritise research that promotes sustainability.

  • The nominee seeks to foster critical thinking skills through active and engaging sessions that encourage collaborative problem solving in relation to sustainability.

  • The nominee promotes careers and external opportunities relating to sustainability within the discipline.

  • The nominee is considerate of the resources they use when delivering content.

 

Are you taught by an academic who is truly innovative in their teaching methods? Someone who seamlessly fuses traditional teaching methods, with pioneering new technologies- all to create a truly personalised learning experience that allows every learning profile to excel. This award looks to commend those academics who strive for teaching excellence. This is not a personality contest, but rather a chance to recognise the value innovative teaching holds within university and wider society.

This award will consider, but is not limited to, the following criteria:

  • The nominee will blend a variety of teaching methods, allowing all learning types to engage with the content as they see fit. This could entail a blend of synchronous and asynchronous teaching methods to make content accessible for all students.

  • The nominee will demonstrate true commitment to continuous professional development, supporting and empowering students to develop beyond their course content.

  • The nominee will demonstrate willingness to adapt and evolve their teaching methods, adopting an agile approach to curriculum design.

  • The nominee is engaging, informative and knowledgeable, encouraging the intellectual development of their students.

  • The nominee provides useful and prompt feedback to students, allowing them too continually better themselves

  • The nominee is approachable and responsive to students and makes them feel valued for their work and contributions.

 

Do you know a lecturer who consistently fosters a productive dialogue with the student body? Someone who continually encourages student feedback, being receptive to their comments and altering the course curriculum in response? This award seeks to commend those academic staff who are committed to the development of student voice within every level of study at NTU. 

This award will consider, but is not limited to, the following criteria:

  • The nominee will demonstrate a continued appreciation for student feedback, recognising its value as a tool for improving student experience.

  • The nominee will facilitate a productive dialogue with the student body, adopting a transparent approach to course development that evidences the role student feedback plays in shaping the course.

  • The nominee will encourage students to engage with the existing feedback channels at NTU. This entails actively promoting the Course Rep and School Officer initiatives, ensuring that students on their course are well-informed on matters of academic representation.

  • The nominee will possess an in-depth understanding of the current academic representative structures present at NTU.

  • The nominee will be highly receptive to student feedback, looking to implement suggestions into their curriculum design.

 

Do you have a research supervisor who is exceptionally supportive of your studies? Do they offer constant encouragement and motivation regarding your research? This is an award for staff members who support postgraduate research projects. 

For this award, we will consider, but not be limited to, the following criteria:

 

  • The nominee will support their students in both their academic and personal life, recognising the importance of the relationship between the two.

  • The nominee challenges their students to contribute new knowledge to their field.

  • The nominee demonstrates continued passion for the area of study, ensuring they are up to date with the latest research and publications.

  • The nominee is agile in their approach to tutoring, demonstrating flexibility as they help their students navigate the challenges of a PhD.

 

Do you have a PhD student who leads your seminar group, providing original ideas and perspectives on your course? Do they bring enthusiasm to their field of study? Perhaps they have offered you mentorship or advised you with your research. This is an award for PhD students who teach and support undergraduate and masters' students.
For this award, we will consider, but not be limited to, the following criteria:

 


  • The nominee will apply their own original research and ideas to their teaching.

  • The nominee is approachable and responsive to students and makes them feel valued for their work and contributions

  • The nominee has enthusiasm for their subject area and inspires this within their students.

  • The nominee is engaging, informative and knowledgeable, therefore ensuring the intellectual development of their students.

  • The nominee provides useful and prompt feedback, allowing students to continually better themselves

  • The nominee bridges the gap between the staff and student communities.
